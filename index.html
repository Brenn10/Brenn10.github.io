General Information

The program

The Beaver Works Summer Institute was created to supply an engineering program for high school students. The program was envisioned and created by Dr. Robert “Bob” Shin, Head of the Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance (ISR) and Tactical Systems Division at MIT Lincoln Laboratory [1]. His vision for this project was to allow high school seniors to have a summer camp that could be brought back to their home high schools.
The ultimate goal for this class over the 4 weeks of lectures, seminars, and labs was to learn to program robots using Robot Operating System (ROS). Each week there was a challenge for each team to accomplish. These events related to what was learned in the previous week. Week one focused on the lidar, a radar system based on visible light, for measuring distance. Week two focused on OpenCV perception and blob detection. Week 3 was intended to be localization, however, it instead turned to exploration. Week 4, the final week, was focused on preparing for the final challenges and race.
